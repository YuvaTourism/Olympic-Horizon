HISTORY OF

WUSHU

History

History of Wushu Origins: Based on Chinese martial arts that are over 2,000 years old, used for self-defense, military combat, and spiritual training. Modern Sport: In the 1950s, China standardized martial arts into Wushu. The International Wushu Federation (IWUF) was founded in 1990, promoting it globally. Forms: Wushu is divided into two main categories: Taolu (routines) and Sanda (sparring). Rules of Wushu Taolu (Forms): Objective: Athletes perform choreographed routines with martial techniques. Scoring: Judges score based on speed, strength, balance, and technical precision. Categories: Bare-handed and weapon-based routines. Time: Performances last 1-2 minutes. Sanda (Sparring): Objective: Two athletes face off in full-contact sparring. Scoring: Points are awarded for punches, kicks, and throws. Rounds: Typically 2 or 3 rounds of 2 minutes. Victory: By points or knockout.
