HISTORY OF

SKATEBOARDING

History

Skateboarding made its Olympic debut at the **2020 Summer Olympics** in Tokyo, marking a significant milestone for the sport as it transitioned from street culture to the global sporting stage. The International Olympic Committee (IOC) included skateboarding in an effort to attract younger audiences and showcase more urban, lifestyle-based sports. Two disciplines were featured: **Street** (which mimics street-style obstacles like rails and stairs) and **Park** (a bowl-shaped course for aerial tricks). Skateboarders competed for medals in both men's and women's categories, with competitors from countries like Japan, Brazil, and the U.S. excelling. The inclusion of skateboarding was widely seen as a success, bringing new energy to the Olympic program and highlighting the sport's creativity and athleticism.
